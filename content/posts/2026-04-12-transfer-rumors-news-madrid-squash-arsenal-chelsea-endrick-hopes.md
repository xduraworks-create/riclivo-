Madrid prefers to keep the Brazilian forward close to home as they plot their next big move, and let’s face it—who can blame them?

Endrick's been on fire in Ligue 1, showing exactly why he’s touted as one of the brightest talents in world football. At 17, the kid is already turning heads and racking up the kind of stats that make scouts drool. He’s not just a kid playing for fun; he’s got skill, speed, and a nose for goal that’s rare at his age. But the reality is, when Real Madrid calls, you answer. Madrid sees a future star in him and isn't about to let him slip through their fingers, especially with pressure mounting to find a suitable backup for Kylian Mbappé.

Let’s break this down. Arsenal and Chelsea, both giants in their own right, were eyeing up Endrick as a potential game-changer for their squads. Can you imagine him weaving past defenders at the Emirates or Stamford Bridge? It would have been a dream for fans. But, much to their dismay, they’ve been given the cold shoulder. Real's management is keeping a tight grip on their asset, deciding instead to bring Endrick back and integrate him into the first team when the time is right. 

This move also signals Madrid’s larger vision. They aren’t just looking for a short-term fix; they want to build for the future. Endrick’s success in Ligue 1 proves he’s ready for the next step, and Madrid is ready to give him that shot. With the likes of Gonzalo García also on their radar as Mbappé’s backup, Madrid is playing chess while others are playing checkers. They know a young talent like Endrick could be crucial as they aim for domestic and European glory.

Meanwhile, Arsenal fans shouldn’t let the four-point gap to Manchester City send them into a panic just yet. Sure, the Gunners have drawn against Brentford, but there’s still plenty of time to regroup. If Arteta can add a player of Endrick's caliber, then we’re talking about a shift in the balance of power in the Premier League. The question is, can they do that without the South American star? It looks unlikely now.

Shifting focus to Chelsea, who are rumored to be scouting for fresh blood, especially after their mixed bag of results this season. Adding Endrick would have been a statement of intent from the Blues, but alas, it seems that ship has sailed. The summer window will be all about big moves and bigger questions, but losing out on a player like Endrick stings for both Arsenal and Chelsea, no matter how you slice it.
