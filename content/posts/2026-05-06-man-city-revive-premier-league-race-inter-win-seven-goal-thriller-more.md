What a weekend it was in the football world! Manchester City is back on track in the Premier League title race, while Inter Milan treated fans to a seven-goal thriller that had everyone talking. It’s moments like these that remind us why we love this beautiful game.

Let’s start with City. They managed to claw their way back into the title conversation, despite what seemed like a wobble earlier in the season. They demolished their opponents, showcasing the attacking flair that has become synonymous with Pep Guardiola's side. The stats are staggering—67% possession in the first half alone! That's City for you, dominating the ball like it’s a personal vendetta. It’s a classic example of their relentless pressure, but it also shows how well they can exploit mistakes. 

Chelsea, on the other hand, played the counter game against Villa—but boy, did it backfire. Even though they had their moments, the defensive frailties reared their ugly heads again. Young Andrey Santos getting brushed aside by Nico O'Reilly led to a cascade of goals that would make any manager cringe. Sure, Rayan Cherki sprinkled some magic dust to set up Marc Guéhi, but let’s not kid ourselves: Chelsea's performance was far from convincing. Another day, another round of boos echoing around Stamford Bridge. 

It’s evident that Chelsea’s roster is a patchwork quilt of talent—some promising, some downright puzzling. With players like Marc Cucurella only able to generate meaningful xG in the dying minutes, the blame game falls hard on the shoulders of manager Liam Rosenior. He’s got to make something out of this ragtag bunch, but how much can any manager do with a squad that often looks like a Frankenstein-like concoction of ‘potential’? 

Now let’s chat about Tottenham. What a way for Roberto De Zerbi to kick off his tenure! A trip to Sunderland, where the atmosphere is electric and the opposition is physical, is like throwing a lamb to the wolves. It was a tough one to watch for Spurs fans as they fell short. De Zerbi didn’t have the magic wand to change their fortunes overnight, and it’s clear he’ll need time to instill his philosophy. But let’s be real—holding onto hope is essential in football. Will he be the manager to finally get Spurs back to where they belong? 

Over in Serie A, Inter Milan turned on the style in a jaw-dropping seven-goal spectacle! Their clash had everything: goals, drama, and a showcase of footballing prowess that had the San Siro buzzing. I mean, who doesn’t love a good ol’ shootout? It was a masterclass in offensive football, and it’s clear that Inter is not messing around this season. They’re a serious contender, and if they keep this form up, they might just stroll to the Scudetto.

Chelsea’s earlier result does keep their title hopes flickering, but let’s not kid ourselves. Down nine points to City, their path to glory looks about as likely as a snowstorm in July. Every time the Blues look like they’re ready to take a step forward, they stumble back with a performance that screams inconsistency. 

As we move forward, all eyes will be on Manchester City. Can they sustain this momentum? If they continue to adapt and overcome, they’ll emerge stronger, and the Premier League is about to witness a thrilling climax. If they keep pressuring teams like they did recently, the title could very well be on its way back to the Etihad.

So, here’s my bold prediction: Manchester City will not only reclaim the Premier League but also march deep into the Champions League, turning doubters into believers. Will Chelsea find their way back into the title race, or is this the beginning of a new era of struggles for them? Let’s get the debate rolling—what’s your take on a thrilling weekend of football?
