The summer transfer window has been buzzing with activity, and as clubs scramble to finalize their squads, we take a keen look at the confirmed deals, the hot rumours heating up, players to keep an eye on, and the deals that sadly fell through. This week, key moves are reshaping the landscape of European football, and there's plenty to discuss.

## Confirmed Deals

Chelsea's ambitious summer reshuffle continues as they have secured the signing of Alejandro Garnacho from Manchester United. The Blues managed to negotiate a deal worth approximately £50 million for the promising winger, who was keen for a fresh start after struggling for game time at Old Trafford. This acquisition is part of a broader strategy to bolster their attacking options ahead of the new season.

Barcelona, meanwhile, confirmed Robert Lewandowski's departure to Al Ahli on a lucrative deal that reportedly sees the Polish striker pocket around €20 million annually. This move marks a significant transition for the Catalan club, which is now looking to rebuild their attack with younger talents.

AC Milan has also been active, securing a new central striker in the form of Giovanni Simeone from Hellas Verona. The deal, worth around €25 million, comes as Milan seeks to improve their scoring options to maintain competitiveness in Serie A and Europe.

In the Premier League, Liverpool has been making headlines as well. They have finalized the signing of midfielder Ryan Gravenberch from Bayern Munich for a fee of €30 million. The Dutch international is expected to bring dynamism and creativity to Liverpool's midfield, enhancing their chances for another title run.

## Hot Rumours

### Jadon Sancho to Borussia Dortmund (HOT)

Rumour has it that Jadon Sancho could be making a return to Dortmund after a disappointing stint at Manchester United. With both clubs reportedly in discussions, this move has a high chance of happening, especially as United seeks to offload players to balance their books. With a credibility rating of HOT, this transfer could rejuvenate Sancho's career significantly.

### Mateo Kovačić to Juventus (WARM)

Juventus are eyeing a move for Chelsea's Mateo Kovačić. The Croatian midfielder’s technical prowess and experience in Serie A make him an appealing target. While the discussions are still early, the credibility rating is WARM, as Juve looks to strengthen their midfield for the upcoming campaign.

### Benoît Badiashile to Newcastle United (COLD)

Newcastle United is reportedly interested in Chelsea defender Benoît Badiashile. However, given Chelsea’s current squad depth and their reluctance to sell, this rumour has a COLD credibility rating. It’s more likely to be a speculative link rather than a concrete move.

### Nicolo Zaniolo to Tottenham Hotspur (WARM)

Tottenham are said to be considering a move for AS Roma's Nicolo Zaniolo, as they look to enhance their attacking options this summer. Zaniolo has been a target for several clubs in Europe, and while no official bid has been made, this rumour holds a WARM credibility rating as Spurs aim to inject some creativity into their frontline.

## Ones to Watch

### Christian Pulisic (AC Milan)

The American winger has been a standout in pre-season and could become a key player for AC Milan this term. His adaptability and skill set make him someone to watch closely as he integrates into the Serie A side.

### Darwin Núñez (Liverpool)

After a strong finish to last season, Núñez is poised for a breakout campaign. His performances in friendlies have been promising, and he might prove pivotal for Liverpool's title ambitions.

### Rasmus Højlund (Manchester United)

The young forward has shown flashes of brilliance during his time at Atalanta. There’s a lot of pressure on him to deliver at United, but his potential suggests he could become a fan favourite.

### Victor Osimhen (Napoli)

Osimhen remains one of the hottest properties in world football after an impressive season in Serie A. Expect plenty of eyes on him as he looks to emulate his previous goal-scoring feats.

## Ones that Fell Through

### Moises Caicedo to Arsenal

Arsenal was heavily linked with a move for Brighton's Moises Caicedo, but the deal collapsed due to financial disagreements. Brighton’s valuation proved too rich for the Gunners, leading to a frustrating end to this transfer saga.

### Paulo Dybala to Chelsea

After initial reports suggested that Dybala could make a switch to Stamford Bridge, talks ultimately broke down over personal terms. The Argentine was keen on a lucrative deal, but Chelsea opted to pursue other targets, leaving Dybala's future uncertain.

### Niko Kovac’s proposed move to Bayer Leverkusen

The former Monaco manager was in talks to join Leverkusen but ultimately chose to remain in Ligue 1 with a different club. This development leaves Bayer in search of a new manager, causing a ripple of instability in their plans.

### Memphis Depay to Atletico Madrid

After strong initial interest, Barcelona and Atletico Madrid could not agree on a fee for the Dutch forward. Depay remains with Barcelona, but his future at the club hangs in the balance as competition for places heats up.

As the transfer window progresses, the dynamics within clubs are constantly evolving. The next few weeks will be crucial as teams finalize their squads ahead of the new season. Fans and analysts alike will be watching closely to see how these moves pan out and what surprises may be in store before the window closes.
