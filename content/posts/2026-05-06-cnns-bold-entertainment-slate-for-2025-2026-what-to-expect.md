Hold onto your popcorn, folks! CNN is stepping up its game in the entertainment arena with some tantalizing new projects set to hit our screens in 2025 and beyond. Yes, you heard that right—CNN is not just about hard news; they’re bringing us riveting documentaries and tantalizing travel shows that promise to make your binge-watching nights even better. 

Let’s talk about the crown jewel of CNN's upcoming offerings: “Searching for France,” hosted by none other than Eva Longoria. This isn’t just another travel show where the host sips wine and eats baguettes; it’s a deep dive into the cultural tapestry of France, exploring its people, cuisine, and arts. Longoria’s sharp insights combined with her passion for food and culture will undoubtedly create a viewing experience as rich as a chocolate fondant. And with a release slated for 2026, you have plenty of time to dust off your beret.

Then there’s “Tony Shalhoub Breaking Bread,” which promises to combine the charm of culinary exploration with heartfelt conversation. Imagine the Emmy-winning actor, known for his roles in “Monk” and “The Marvelous Mrs. Maisel,” sharing meals with fascinating guests. Food has a unique way of bridging gaps and unearthing stories, and Shalhoub is the perfect host to guide us through this delightful journey. 

Anderson Cooper and Andy Cohen will be back for CNN’s New Year’s Eve Live in 2026, and let's face it—this duo never fails to deliver a night of laughter, nostalgia, and those delightfully awkward moments that keep us coming back year after year. In a world filled with divisive content, can we take a moment to appreciate how they manage to make our New Year's celebrations entertaining and relatable? Their chemistry will undoubtedly light up the screen again, making us feel like we’re part of the festivities. 

CNN is also looking to broaden its horizons with a slew of documentaries. “I’m Chevy Chase and You’re Not” promises an unfiltered look at the ups and downs of one of Hollywood's most polarizing figures. Directed by the brilliant Marina Zenovich, this doc could possibly redeem Chase’s legacy or plunge it further into the murky waters of Hollywood gossip. What will we learn about a man who’s both beloved and controversial? One thing’s certain—this will be a wild ride.

Speaking of wild rides, the festival darling “Prime Minister,” which has already won awards at Sundance, joins the CNN Films lineup. This documentary is set to explore the ever-complicated world of politics, and it will likely resonate in an era where the lines between celebrity and politician are increasingly blurred. Get ready for some jaw-dropping revelations that could change how we see our leaders.

And let’s not forget the layers of celebrity gossip and drama that always keep us riveted. Take Maksim Chmerkovskiy and his recent call-out of Kirstie Alley over divisive comments regarding the Ukraine war. While both are notable figures from "Dancing With The Stars," this public spat serves as a reminder that celebrities are not just entertainers; they are also voices in contentious debates. It’s captivating and uncomfortable, but it’s the kind of drama we can’t look away from.

As for the celebrity sightings that keep our Instagram feeds lively, Celine Dion continues to dazzle—and no, she’s not just the queen of power ballads; she's a cultural icon. Meanwhile, stars like Zendaya and Colman Domingo are effortlessly captivating in "Euphoria," a show that raises important discussions about mental health and adolescence. Their performances are so raw, they remind us of the incredibly complex nature of growing up in today’s world.

And don’t sleep on the musical talent that’s rising to the top. Megan Thee Stallion and Klay Thompson’s recent appearance together was not just a moment for basketball and hip-hop fans; it was a cultural statement about collaboration across industries. We need to keep our eyes peeled for more moments that blend sports with celebrity culture, as this seems to be a trend that's here to stay.

As CNN gears up for these remarkable projects, it’s clear they’re diversifying their content to keep us entertained while still informing us. The days of being just a news network are gone; they’re stepping into the ring with the heavyweights of entertainment. 

Will CNN manage to carve out a permanent place in our entertainment rotation, or will they struggle to keep up with platforms like Netflix and Hulu? One thing’s for sure: if they keep delivering content like this, we’ll be tuning into CNN for more than just breaking news. What do you think—are we ready for CNN to be our new go-to for entertainment?
