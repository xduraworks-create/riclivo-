It's no secret that CNN isn’t just about breaking news and political debates anymore. The network is diving headfirst into the entertainment pool with ambitious new projects that could redefine its cultural relevance. Picture it: "Searching for France" and "Tony Shalhoub Breaking Bread" are just the tip of the iceberg, and they have the potential to draw both casual viewers and hard-core fans in droves.

Let’s talk about "Searching for France." This series promises a delectable mix of travel, culture, and cuisine, featuring the complexities and charms of France like never before. With the rise of culinary travel shows dominating our screens, CNN seems poised to capture that wanderlust spirit while giving us a taste of French culture—literally. Can you imagine a segment where a Michelin-starred chef takes us behind the scenes at his restaurant? If done right, this could be more than just another food show; it could become a cultural phenomenon.

Then there's "Tony Shalhoub Breaking Bread," which is already sounding like a cozy romp through culinary delights. Shalhoub, known for his compelling performances in "Monk" and "The Marvelous Mrs. Maisel," invites viewers to gather around the table—literally. Imagine that blend of food and heartfelt conversation; it’s bound to appeal to the emotional side of us all. This show could help elevate CNN's profile in lifestyle programming, making it not just a news outlet but a staple for lifestyle enthusiasts.

But wait, there’s more! CNN is also exploring the serious side of storytelling with an upcoming documentary about the legendary Chevy Chase. This isn’t just another Hollywood bio; it promises to peel back the layers of Chase’s tumultuous career. From "Saturday Night Live" to blockbuster films, Chase’s influence has been indelible. We can expect interviews with colleagues, archival footage, and a deep dive into the man behind the comedian. Here’s hoping they tackle both the highs and lows of his career—because who doesn’t love a comeback story?

On a more somber note, CNN will also be releasing documentaries focused on cancers in young adults. This timely project addresses a critical issue that doesn't get nearly enough airtime. While we love our popcorn flicks, there’s something to be said for media that sparks important conversations. This documentary could serve as a wake-up call for many and might even move the needle in terms of advocacy and awareness.

And if you thought CNN was just sitting on its laurels, think again. They've acquired the documentary "Prime Minister" centered around the controversial figure Alexei Navalny. In a world where political and social commentary is more than just entertainment, this could offer unprecedented access to the realities of Russian politics. The intrigue surrounding Navalny's life and activism makes for riveting viewing and might just ignite discussions around democracy and oppression. 

Let’s not overlook the recent drama swirling around former “Dancing With The Stars” contestant Kirstie Alley. Maksim Chmerkovskiy called her out on Twitter regarding her controversial comments about the Ukraine war. While this may not directly correlate with CNN’s new projects, it paints a picture of just how quickly the celebrity landscape can shift. With tensions high and opinions polarizing, we can expect more celebrity spats in the spotlight—but let’s hope for a little more grace and a lot less drama.

As we gear up for 2025-2026, it's fascinating to see how CNN is evolving from a news network into a multi-faceted entertainment powerhouse. Sure, we may still tune in for breaking news, but there's something exciting about realizing we can also enjoy quality programming that engages our hearts and minds.

Will these new ventures hit the mark, or will they fizzle out like last season's trends? I have a strong feeling that shows like "Searching for France" and "Tony Shalhoub Breaking Bread" could become instant classics, while the heavy-hitting documentaries give us the substance we crave in a often superficial entertainment landscape.
