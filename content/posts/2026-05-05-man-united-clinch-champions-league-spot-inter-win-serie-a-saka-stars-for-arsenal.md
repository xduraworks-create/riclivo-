What a weekend for football fans across Europe! With Manchester United securing their spot back in the Champions League, Inter Milan celebrating Serie A triumph, and Bukayo Saka reminding everyone why he’s Arsenal’s golden boy, this was a matchday to remember. Let’s break it down.

First off, Manchester United’s victory over arch-rivals Liverpool was not just a win; it was a statement. United has often been criticized for inconsistency, but this display showcased their potential to compete at the highest level again. Bruno Fernandes, the maestro of Old Trafford, registered yet another game where he created more chances than anyone else, proving that he’s not just a luxury but a necessity for this team. The 2-1 win feels like a weight lifted off their shoulders, solidifying their return to Europe’s elite after a season of chaos. 

On the other side of the rivalry, Liverpool’s season has been more of a rollercoaster than a joyride. The loss against United epitomized their struggles—fragile defensively and lacking that cutting edge in the final third. What’s their plan moving forward? Jürgen Klopp needs to address the midfield issues and fix the leaky defense if they want to be in contention next season. 

Let’s switch gears to Italy, where Inter Milan clinched their 21st Serie A title in a season that commenced with heavy doubt. After a tumultuous summer filled with uncertainties, Christian Chivu has proven himself to be a master of rebuilding. Losing key players like Lautaro Martínez and Hakan Çalhanoglu for significant periods tested the squad’s depth, yet Inter has shown remarkable resilience. Only losing one game since November is no small feat and speaks volumes of the unity and tactical discipline Chivu has instilled. 

The fact that Inter not only dominated domestically but also reached the Coppa Italia final is a testament to Chivu’s managerial prowess. Let’s not forget, this isn’t just another title; it’s the culmination of hard work and strategic decisions made on a smaller budget. Chivu has set a blueprint on how to compete with Italy's heavyweights, and it’ll be fascinating to see how he builds on this success in the coming seasons.

Now, onto the Premier League, where Arsenal's 2-0 thumping of Fulham highlighted the Gunners' title credentials. But this performance was more than just about the scoreline—it was about Bukayo Saka returning to the pitch. With a goal and an assist, Saka was electric, and it’s no surprise that Arsenal looked like a different side with him back in the mix. His direct contribution to 150 goals in his career at just 24 is astounding. Arsenal’s attack flows smoother with him, and with six points clear of Manchester City, they are putting pressure on the reigning champions.

Mikel Arteta must be licking his lips at the prospect of what this squad can achieve, especially if Saka remains fit. Can he be the catalyst that pushes Arsenal to their first Premier League title since 2004? With the Champions League on the horizon, they’ll need Saka’s creativity to break down defenses across Europe. 

Let’s not gloss over the relegation battle either. Tottenham’s 2-1 win over Aston Villa might not seem monumental, but it’s crucial for a team that’s been battling the drop. Ryan Mason’s appointment has been a breath of fresh air, and if they can maintain this momentum, they might just secure safety. The Premier League is as unpredictable as ever, with teams like Wrexham also feeling the heat of promotion dreams dashed, which just adds to the drama.

As we look ahead, the European competitions are heating up. Arsenal’s form suggests they could be serious contenders in the Champions League, while United has regained some swagger that could unsettle any opponent. Inter Milan, fresh off their title win, will undoubtedly be looking to carry this momentum into Europe next season as well.

What’s your take on these developments? Will Saka propel Arsenal to glory, or will Manchester United build on their Champions League return to challenge for the title? And can Inter Milan keep this winning formula going amid tougher competition next year? Let’s hear your thoughts!
