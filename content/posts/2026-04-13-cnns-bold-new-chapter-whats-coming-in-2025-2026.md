As we bid adieu to 2025, CNN is doing more than ringing in the New Year with confetti and cocktails. They’re stepping into the future with a lineup of new programming destined to shake up our screens like never before. Whether it’s the high-energy performances from Brandy, Monica, and Shakira at their New Year's Eve special or fresh documentary series that promise to captivate, CNN is ready to redefine what we expect from news and entertainment. 

While 2025 was a year of significant mergers and acquisitions in Hollywood, CNN has taken a distinctly different path, focusing on unique storytelling that blends information and entertainment. The announcement of new series and documentaries is not just about filling airtime; it’s about creating content that resonates with viewers’ interests and concerns. Eva Longoria’s "Searching For France" is set to continue the Emmy-winning franchise in 2026, and it’s hard not to feel excited about where she will take us next. Expect delicious cuisine and rich culture, all served with a side of Longoria’s charm.

Then there's the intriguing "Tony Shalhoub Breaking Bread” series premiering this fall. Imagine the culinary journeys that await us through the lens of the Emmy-winning actor! This isn’t just about food; it’s a cultural exploration that promises to blend history, travel, and, of course, food. Shalhoub's unique perspective will draw viewers in and keep them hungry for more.

And what’s a network without its documentaries? CNN Films is rolling out two new features—"I’m Chevy Chase and You’re Not," which sounds like the perfect dose of nostalgia, and "In the Prime of Life," a timely exploration of rising cancer rates in adults under 50. The latter, directed by Janet Tobias, is essential viewing in a world where health concerns are at the forefront of our minds. It’s a bold move that combines entertainment with advocacy in a way that only CNN can.

While the programming slate has us buzzing, let’s talk about the New Year's Eve 2026 special. Anderson Cooper and Andy Cohen have made it a tradition to guide us into the New Year with laughs, music, and a lineup of celebrity guests that reads like a who's who of pop culture. Performers like Bryan Adams and the dynamic duo of Brandy and Monica will undoubtedly create a memorable atmosphere. And let's not forget the surprise guests—they always deliver something unexpected, keeping us all on our toes.

This special isn’t just a countdown; it’s a celebration of our shared culture and the moments that define us. Whether you’re a fan of late-night humor or live performances, there’s something for everyone. Celebrity appearances from Stephen Colbert to Patti LaBelle ensure that we’re in for a night of unfiltered entertainment. I mean, who doesn’t want to see a surprise duet or a hilarious anecdote shared among friends?

With titles like "The Last Movie Stars," which dives into the lives of Paul Newman and Joanne Woodward, CNN is proving that they still hold the keys to compelling storytelling. The documentary team consistently brings their A-game, and the Emmy-winning pedigree of their projects is a testament to that. Remember "Anthony Bourdain Parts Unknown"? That series touched hearts and opened minds, setting a high bar for all subsequent food and travel shows. 

Yet, amidst this buzz and excitement, we can't ignore the shadows. The entertainment industry is no stranger to controversy. Recently, Maksim Chmerkovskiy’s public confrontation with Kirstie Alley over her comments on the war in Europe brought to light how celebrities' voices can influence public opinion—both positively and negatively. It’s a reminder that with fame comes responsibility, and we often find ourselves grappling with the line between personal views and public platforms.

As we look forward to a thrilling 2026, the entertainment landscape continues to evolve, driven by bold storytelling and iconic performances. CNN seems poised to reclaim its place as a leader in this space, with programming that not only informs but also entertains. 

So what do you think? Is CNN ready to dominate the entertainment scene, or is it merely a flash in the pan? With all this exciting content, one thing's clear: 2026 is bound to be a wild ride, and we’re ready to buckle up!
