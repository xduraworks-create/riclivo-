Did you hear that in 2025, the animated sensation "Ne Zha 2" soared above all competition to become the biggest global box office hit? It’s a testament to the power of storytelling, and let’s be real, the world was ready for a mythological adventure that resonated with audiences far and wide. If Hollywood has been living in the shadows of franchise fatigue, maybe it’s time to give animated films more credit.

Meanwhile, the cinematic landscape saw "Anora" sweep the Oscars with five golden statuettes, reminding us all that poignant storytelling and mesmerizing performances can still captivate hearts and minds. The film's lead, Jessie Buckley, who snagged the Oscar for Best Actress, is proving to be a powerhouse. With her remarkable range and ability to convey raw emotion, she’s not just an actress; she’s a force of nature.

But let's not overlook the major shakeups happening behind the scenes. Skydance Media, a titan in the industry, made waves by acquiring Paramount Global, setting the stage for a new era of content creation and distribution. This move could mean some incredible collaborations—or a whole lot of corporate drama. And as if that wasn’t enough, Amazon MGM Studios now has the reins of the "James Bond" franchise. Buckle up, because James might just be taking a different turn with the likes of Jeff Bezos at the helm.

Disney, never one to be outdone, wrapped up its acquisition of NBCUniversal's Hulu stake. The streaming wars are heating up, and we’re here for every plot twist. With so many platforms vying for our attention, it’s becoming a golden age for content consumers. But will it lead to content overload? As we dive deeper into this era, we need to ask ourselves—how much is too much?

Speaking of new content, CNN has unveiled an exciting slate of programming. We’ve got Eva Longoria diving into French culture with "Searching for France," set to debut in 2026. This tantalizing exploration promises to serve up both delicious pastry and cultural insights—who wouldn't want to tune in for that? Then there’s Tony Shalhoub's "Breaking Bread," fusing food and travel into a delectable series we didn’t know we needed. 

Let’s not forget about the documentaries. A certain title, "I’m Chevy Chase and You’re Not," will undoubtedly stir some nostalgia and perhaps a few cringe moments. Given Chevy’s tumultuous history, this documentary directed by two-time Emmy winner Marina Zenovich will be an unfiltered look at a comedy legend’s life. 

And then there’s "Prime Minister," which raked in the Audience Award at Sundance 2025. Collaborating with Magnolia Pictures and HBO Documentary Films, this could be a groundbreaker as it dives into political landscapes—always a hot topic that sparks debate. 

The celebrity world never sleeps, and neither do the controversies. Maksim Chmerkovskiy calling out Kirstie Alley for her insensitive remarks on Twitter about the war in Ukraine was a stark reminder that even beloved stars shouldn't escape accountability. It’s a lesson we all need: our words matter, especially in times of crisis.

In the realm of celebrity sightings and accolades, we have Rihanna gracing the New York scene and Taylor Frankie Paul dazzling at the Oscars. These moments are more than just red carpet walks; they signify the cultural touchstones that shape our conversations. And let’s not forget Jim Carrey, who received an honorary César award this year. His comedic genius continues to inspire, and it’s about time Hollywood recognizes long-standing talent.

However, it’s impossible to ignore the sadder news. The passing of Oliver "Power" Grant, a longtime collaborator of Wu-Tang Clan, at just 52 is a stark reminder of the fragility of life. His contributions to hip-hop culture will never fade, and his loss will be felt deeply.
